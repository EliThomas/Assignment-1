Project Idea

Puberty App

The project I am planning to do is an application that will be able to be used on any phone or computer (so any website or phone application on Android or IOs). This app will be designed as a ‘Puberty App’ for anyone going through puberty. It will detail any and all activities, facts and tips for people of any gender going through puberty. This includes, but is not limited to; shaving (leg, armpits, chest, and face), skin care routines, bras, body care routines, sexual care (birth control, vasectomy, and condoms), periods, erections, mood swings. It will also include an LGBTQIA+ section that will help everyone of any gender and/or sexual identity.

A ‘Puberty App’ such as this one is a really big idea for me and has been for a few years as I never had a massive help from my parents or any other guardian figure on how to do things. Then as I came out as transgender and found so many young people who have to go through those changes alone, I tried to help in any possible way I could. 45,714. According to The Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW) that is the number of children who have been abused and don’t have a proper guardian in Australia.v An app of this significance could be a life changer for these young people as they’ll have something to refer to for questions and things about puberty that can be quite difficult and confusing.

Within the program of this ‘Puberty App’ key activities, facts and tips will be included; this describes the transformation that happens with estrogen, testosteronevi, gender transforming surgeries, sexual health and any other needs that come in later updates.

For anyone undergoing transformation through estrogen (whether that be cisgender or transfeminine) there will be information on:

  • Bras; sizing, different types (Underwired, push-up, balconette, bralette, strapless, tube, sports, t-shirt, beginner’s, transparent, bridal, multiway, plunge, cage, halter, nursing, stick-on, full figure, padded, minimizer and racerback)
  • Sexual health care; birth control (both the pill and the rod), condoms (why they’re important and how to use one)
  • Periods; how they work and what they do, pads and tampons (the pros and cons for both and the different brands)
  • Mood swings; the most common moods associated with estrogen.

    • Anyone undergoing transformation through testosterone (whether that be cisgender or transmasculine) there will be information on:

  • Development of testicles “balls dropping”.
  • Wet dreams.
  • Erections; their function and general information.
  • Sexual health care; vasectomy (function and information) and condoms (how and when to use and what they do).
  • Voice breaks.
  • Mood swings; the most common moods associated with testosterone.

    • For both estrogen and testosterone there will also be information on:

  • Shaving; face, legs, under arms and chest (razors information, how-to videos, and step-by-step instructions), hair removal (waxing, cream, shaving cream, laser, epilators, plucking).
  • Skin care routines; creams, moisturizers, anti-acne etc. (different brands and uses).

    • The LGBTQIA+ area will include information for both transfeminine and transmasculine.

    For transfeminine it will include:

  • Padding; sizing and how-to (padded bras and underwear)
  • Body shaping; types (control top pantyhose, camisole, waist cincher)
  • Gaffs; types (underwear, adhesive tape)
  • Hormone effects (changes to face, increase in chest, decrease in genitals, changing of body shape, voice, mood swings)
  • Surgeries; top surgery (breast implants), bottom surgery (Penile inversion, rectosigmoid or colon graft, non-penile inversion vaginoplasty).

    • For transmasculine it will include:

  • Binders; sizing and types (half, full, t-shirt, singlet, swimming)
  • Packers; sizing and types (STP, soft, hard)
  • Hormone effects (changes to face, reduction of chest, changing of body shape, voice, bottom growth, mood swings)
  • Surgeries; top surgery (Buttonhole, double incision, inverted-T/T-anchor, peri-areolar, keyhole, minimal scar), xibottom surgery (Metoidioplasty, phalloplasty).

    • For both gender identification and sexual orientation there will be information on:

  • Different types of gender identification (Cisgender, transgender, gender nonconforming, nonbinary, genderqueer, genderfluid, gender-neutral, intersex)
  • Different types of sexual orientation (Straight, gay, lesbian, bisexual, pansexual, asexual, demisexual, graysexual)
  • How to come out to your parents.
  • What to do if your parents don’t accept what you say.
  • LGBTQIA+ lines.

    • In saying all of that, this app is not just for teenagers or those part of the LGBTQIA+, this app will be able to have use for parents and people who are generally curious. There will be an option to create an account or stay anonymous while still being able to save articles or ask questions.

    There will also be an area that is safe for people to report abuse or neglect on anyone and have the anonymity around it. There will be a very specific area around mental health that will have trigger warnings surrounded it.
