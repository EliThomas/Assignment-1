    What is your interest in IT?

    I have always had a knack for technology and the older I got the more I found myself relying on each piece I had. I currently own 3 monitors, 3 keyboards, 2 mice, 2 gaming desktop towers, an Xbox One S, PlayStation 4, Nintendo Switch, Nintendo DS lite, Nintendo 3DS XL and 5 laptops in various states of usability. I have collected all of this over the last 10 years and will probably continue to grow. As well as my love for technology I have always felt the drive to find out exactly how things work, this including opening up computers to see how they run and teaching myself as much as I can with my limited time about each part and how to make them better. This then turned into wanting to know how apps are created, and then websites and it grew from there. All of this interest led me to starting this course so I could learn as much as I could about it all.

    Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

    I chose RMIT as I read that it was one of the best universities in Australia for Technology, ranking in the top 10 at topuniversities.com. My sister also begun her studies here back in 2019 in law (has since dropped out) and I have heard many great things about RMIT. Most of my knowledge of the school has come from word of mouth and then little research. I found RMIT through Open Universities Australia and it was the top result in searching for Bachelor of IT.

    What do you expect to learn during your studies?

    As per the study guide on Canvas, I expect to learn how to build websites, different types of jobs within the IT industry, multiple platforms that I can use to connect with other IT developers, use of the Cloud, Mobile Technologies, AR and VR, Networking, and a dash of robotics.
